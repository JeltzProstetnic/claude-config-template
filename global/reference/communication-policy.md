# Communication Policy — Conditional Reference

Loaded when drafting emails, social posts, or formal correspondence.

## Drafting Rules

1. **Match the user's voice.** Study previous sent messages (Gmail) before drafting. Mirror their tone, sentence structure, and level of formality.
2. **Never send without approval.** All drafts are presented for review. The user decides when to send.
3. **Language follows recipient.** Default to the language the recipient uses. Ask if ambiguous.
4. **Professional hierarchy awareness.** Adjust formality based on relationship: peers get casual, superiors/strangers get formal, academics get field-appropriate register.
5. **No corporate speak.** Avoid buzzwords, filler phrases, and hollow enthusiasm. Be direct and genuine.
6. **Context over template.** Every email is situational. Don't apply a formula — read the thread, understand the relationship, draft accordingly.

## Social Media Posts

1. **Platform-native style.** LinkedIn ≠ Twitter ≠ email. Match the platform's conventions.
2. **Authentic voice.** The post should sound like the user wrote it, not like an AI generated it.
3. **No hashtag spam.** Use hashtags sparingly and only when they add discoverability value.

## Information Capture

When drafting reveals new contact information, relationships, or context about people in the user's network, flag it for capture in the appropriate knowledge base (relationships, people DB, etc.).
